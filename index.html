About

Computational neuroscience is the intersection of order and chaos. The brain is an extraordinarily complex system, yet distinct patterns and recognizable rhythms arise from its dynamic activity. I am drawn to this tension: The idea that thought, while seemingly abstract, may be modeled, simulated and understood through computational systems.

My engagement in the development of such networks and simulations attempts to address this area of tension. I study Computer Science with a minor in Psychology, and a semester abroad at Freie Universität Berlin deepened my interest in how systems behave. I am an IEEE member and I also serve as Fundraising Chair for SWE. Beyond academics, I have spent years in IT management, led technical training programs, earned certification in Neuroscience, and am actively working towards certification in Generative AI Engineering. I am eager to advance my education through meaningful experiences and engagements in opportunities for collaboration, education and employment.
